As the sun started to rise and wash away the fog she noticed the ghosts started growing fainter. The lights on the doodad started to flutter. It was running out of juice. She kept flicking through but it was all the same thing: the same corpse jumping from place to place.

Then she pressed the button and there was no corpse. Was there something coming up the steps to the bandstand? She squinted. Sure enough: a fuzzy thing; she had to go up to it to see it clearly. It was a ghost self but different. This one was wearing clean jeans and a tank top and her skin though still a little blistered and scabbed looked a little plumper like it was skin and not paper. The ghost folded her arms on the railing of the bandstand and watched the magenta sunrise splash over the park. She then took a medicine bottle from her pocket labelled “Retrovir” and swallowed a tablet from it. She looked tired but peaceful. Maggie’d seen many sunrises from long nights out but couldn’t remember looking at one that way before. Like it wasn’t a burden.

Maggie looked at the device. The lights were barely on now. The ghost was blending into the air. If she pressed the other button she would take the ghost’s place in that reality. But then again she and this other Maggie were still standing here. Whatever was in that other one that allowed her to pick life over death was also inside herself.

Maggie turned away from the ghost spat out the syringe and stepped on it. She put the doodad on the railing and stepped out of the bandstand. The tint of magenta was now turning amber like a honey jar. The grass in the park was dewy like hair after a shower. She heard the first buses cars and birds. The city was yawning.

The bug might kill me she thought but it won’t beat me.
